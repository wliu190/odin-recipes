This is my classic lasagne recipe! First inspired by Bon Appetit, it now just takes whatever form I fancy.

For this recipe, you'll need:

  • Mince (500g)
  • Bacon (optional, finely diced)
  • Carrot (one, finely diced)
  • Onion (one, finely diced)
  • Celery (one, finely diced)
  • Tomato paste
  • Tinned chopped tomatoes (350 g)
  • Milk
  • Flour
  • Lasagne sheets

The recipe consists of three stages: making the ragu, making the bechamel, and finally, assembly.

Making the meat sauce

This component takes the longest, but is where all the flavour comes from!

  1. Fry off the bacon, then the mince
  2. Add onion, carrot, and celery and fry off
  3. Deglaze with white wine, then add tomato paste
  4. Add beef stock and milk to cover, add herbs
  5. Simmer for at least an hour

Making the bechamel sauce

The bechamel sauce is nothing special, but you can add parmesan to make it better!

  1. Melt a knob of butter
  2. Add a tablespoon or two of flour, fry off
  3. Slowly whisk in milk, adding 500 mL or so
  4. Bring to boil, then let simmer to thicken
  5. Remove from heat (add cheese now if desired)

Assembly

This is the fun part! Start with a layer of ragu and bechamel and layer lasagne sheets over. Repeat this process until you've run out of something (or space!). The last layer should be lasagne sheets with bechamel on top, sprinkled with parmesan.

Put in the oven for 45 mins at 180C. Let cool for 15 minutes (essential!) and enjoy!
